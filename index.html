CISA confirms active exploitation of Lantronix EDS5000 serial-to-IP servers — unauthenticated root on the box that bridges legacy OT to the network

The most urgent OT item this week: CISA has confirmed in-the-wild exploitation of CVE-2025-67038, a maximum-severity (CVSS 9.8) OS command-injection flaw in Lantronix EDS5000 serial-to-IP device servers, and added it to the Known Exploited Vulnerabilities catalog as part of a four-CVE batch. An unauthenticated attacker can inject arbitrary OS commands — executed as root — and because these converters are precisely what plants use to put serial PLCs, meters and field gear onto an Ethernet network, a takeover sits directly astride the IT/OT boundary. The flaw traces to Forescout's BRIDGE:BREAK research, and this week's ICS Patch Tuesday added fresh CISA advisories for Lantronix EDS3000PS and EDS5000. Treat any internet-reachable EDS5000 as presumed-compromised, pull it off the public internet now, patch, and threat-hunt the OT segment behind it.

High Incident

Anubis ransomware crippled the Adriatic Port Authority — a maritime hit that needed no OT access

A Resecurity case study (published 11 June) details the Anubis ransomware attack on Italy's Adriatic Sea Port Authority (port of Ancona): attackers entered via a spear-phishing email against a port-management contractor, moved laterally through insecure Microsoft 365/Azure accounts, then encrypted the IT systems behind cargo tracking, shipping schedules and customs processing and exfiltrated contracts, employee records and — more sensitively — port safety and security-operations plans. Vessels had to reroute to other ports, driving millions in losses, on a roughly $10M bitcoin demand. The pointed lesson for ports and vessel operators: the attackers never touched OT — pure IT weaknesses produced the operational shutdown, which is exactly the IT/OT-interdependency risk maritime cyber rules are written to address.

Cal Water: Mandiant finds no IT or OT breach after Iran-linked Handala's water-disruption claim

California Water Service disclosed that the Iran-aligned hacktivist group Handala gained unauthorized access to accounts on two third-party service-provider platforms and leaked roughly 5 GB of data. Google's Mandiant investigated and concluded the activity did not reach Cal Water's own IT or OT environments and produced no service impact, despite Handala's public claim it could disrupt the water supply. The episode is a clean reminder that hacktivist “we breached a water utility” claims routinely outrun the facts — and that fast, evidence-based forensics and messaging matter as much as the technical containment when nation-state-aligned actors target the water sector through supply-chain access.

Dragos 2026 OT/ICS Year in Review: ransomware on industrials up 49%, AI-assisted intrusions arrive, adversaries map the control loop

Dragos's newly released annual report finds ransomware groups hitting industrial organizations grew roughly 49% year over year, with more than 3,300 victims and manufacturing absorbing the brunt. Two threads stand out for OT defenders. First, adversaries are moving past mere persistence to actively learn the physics of a process — exfiltrating configuration files, logic and alarm data to understand how the plant runs — raising the ceiling on potential physical impact. Second, the report documents the first AI-assisted intrusions reaching toward OT, including a municipal water-and-drainage utility case where attackers leveraged commercial LLMs during an enterprise-IT compromise that escalated toward the OT boundary. The practical takeaway is unchanged but more urgent: segment IT from OT, kill internet-exposed devices, monitor east-west traffic, and rehearse manual recovery.

CVE-2026-12569, a critical RCE in PTC Windchill and FlexPLM, has been confirmed exploited in the wild and added to CISA's KEV catalog (25 June batch) with an urgent federal remediation deadline. Critical Advisory

Splunk Enterprise RCE exploited in attacks days after disclosure — a blind-spot risk for OT monitoring

SecurityWeek reports that CVE-2026-20253, a critical unauthenticated remote-code-execution flaw in Splunk Enterprise, is being exploited in attacks just days after disclosure; CISA added it to the KEV catalog on 18 June. The bug stems from a missing authentication check on a sidecar recovery endpoint reachable through Splunk Web, and attackers can chain file-write primitives to execute code as the Splunk service account — enabling log tampering, credential theft and lateral movement. Because Splunk is the security-telemetry hub for many industrial operators, its compromise can blind defenders to OT activity and clear the path for deeper intrusion, making this a monitoring-integrity problem rather than just an IT patch.

Schneider Electric PowerLogic P7 — OS command injection on a protection-and-control relay (CVSS up to 7.5)

CISA advisory ICSA-26-176-07 covers multiple flaws in Schneider's PowerLogic P7 protection-and-control relay (CVE-2026-9716/-9717/-9718), including an OS command-injection weakness that can allow execution of commands with elevated privileges when a privileged, authenticated user reaches a network-exposed service — alongside denial-of-service conditions. CVSS tops out around 7.5 (High), but the consequence on a protection relay is what matters: command execution or a forced outage on this class of device can mean loss of visibility or control over part of the electrical network. Schneider has released fixed firmware (V02.004.001); where you can't patch immediately, restrict access to the P7 service ports (8080/3702), apply least privilege, and watch for anomalous requests.

The revised NERC CIP-012-2 standard becomes enforceable on 1 July, now two days out, strengthening requirements to protect the confidentiality and integrity of real-time assessment and monitoring data exchanged between control centers and closing gaps the earlier version left around how that inter-control-center traffic is secured. For bulk-electric-system operators it is an immediate compliance milestone; for everyone else it is a useful reminder that control-center-to-control-center links are a prime target and deserve encryption, integrity protection and monitoring regardless of regulatory scope.
