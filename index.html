CISA confirms PTC Windchill RCE is being exploited in the wild — the PLM backbone that holds engineering drawings and pivots toward the shop floor

The most urgent OT-adjacent item this week: on 25 June CISA added CVE-2026-12569 — a critical (CVSS 9.3) improper-input-validation flaw enabling unauthenticated remote code execution in PTC Windchill PDMLink and FlexPLM — to its Known Exploited Vulnerabilities catalog, setting a 28 June federal remediation deadline. That deadline has now passed and exploitation is continuing: researchers reported as recently as 29 June that attackers are still dropping JSP web shells onto unpatched, internet-reachable instances. Windchill is the product-lifecycle-management hub for automotive, aerospace, defense and other discrete manufacturers, so a compromise exposes engineering drawings, bills of material and supply-chain data — and gives an attacker a credible pivot toward connected OT and shop-floor systems. Treat any internet-facing Windchill/FlexPLM instance as presumed-targeted: patch immediately, hunt for unexpected JSP files and web shells, remove internet exposure, rotate credentials, and review what OT and supplier data the PLM tier can reach.

High Incident

Anubis ransomware crippled the Adriatic Port Authority — a maritime hit that needed no OT access

A Resecurity case study details the Anubis ransomware attack on Italy's Adriatic Sea Port Authority (port of Ancona): attackers entered via a spear-phishing email against a port-management contractor, moved laterally through insecure Microsoft 365/Azure accounts, then encrypted the IT systems behind cargo tracking, shipping schedules and customs processing and exfiltrated contracts, employee records and — more sensitively — port safety and security-operations plans. Vessels had to reroute to other ports, driving millions in losses, on a roughly $10M bitcoin demand. The pointed lesson for ports and vessel operators: the attackers never touched OT — pure IT weaknesses produced the operational shutdown, which is exactly the IT/OT-interdependency risk maritime cyber rules are written to address.

Medium Incident

Cal Water: Mandiant finds no IT or OT breach after Iran-linked Handala's water-disruption claim

California Water Service disclosed that the Iran-aligned hacktivist group Handala gained unauthorized access to accounts on third-party service-provider platforms and leaked roughly 5 GB of data tied to utilities serving Bakersfield, Visalia and Chico. Google's Mandiant investigated and concluded the activity stayed in a GPS-correction server and a billing database and did not reach Cal Water's own IT or OT environments or affect service, despite Handala's public claim it could disrupt the water supply. The episode is a clean reminder that hacktivist “we breached a water utility” claims routinely outrun the facts — and that fast, evidence-based forensics and messaging matter as much as containment when nation-state-aligned actors target the water sector through supply-chain access.

Lantronix EDS5000 serial-to-IP servers exploited — unauthenticated root on the box that bridges legacy OT to the network

CISA added CVE-2025-67038, a maximum-severity (CVSS 9.8) OS command-injection flaw in Lantronix EDS5000 serial-to-IP device servers, to the Known Exploited Vulnerabilities catalog on 23 June after confirming in-the-wild exploitation. An unauthenticated attacker can inject arbitrary OS commands that execute as root, and because these converters are precisely what plants use to put serial PLCs, meters and field gear onto an Ethernet network, a takeover sits directly astride the IT/OT boundary. The flaw traces to Forescout's BRIDGE:BREAK research, and this month's ICS advisories also covered related Lantronix EDS3000PS/EDS5000 issues. Treat any internet-reachable EDS5000 as presumed-compromised, pull it off the public internet, patch, and threat-hunt the OT segment behind it.

High Advisory

Schneider Electric PowerLogic P7 — OS command injection on a protection-and-control relay

CISA advisory ICSA-26-176-07 (25 June) covers multiple flaws in Schneider's PowerLogic P7 protection-and-control platform (CVE-2026-9716/-9717/-9718), including an OS command-injection weakness that can allow unauthorized command execution, alongside denial-of-service conditions. The consequence on a protection relay is what matters: command execution or a forced outage on this class of device can mean loss of visibility or control over part of the electrical network. Schneider has released fixed firmware (V02.004.001); where you can't patch immediately, restrict access to the P7 service ports (8080/3702), enforce least privilege, and alert on anomalous SOAP/management requests targeting wsApp.

High Advisory

B&R Industrial Automation Linux kernel flaws — local privilege escalation on APROL and X20E edge controllers

CISA advisory ICSA-26-174-06 covers multiple Linux kernel vulnerabilities in B&R (ABB) products, affecting Linux for B&R 12 and earlier, APROL process-control systems and X20E embedded/edge controllers. The most notable issue, CVE-2026-31431, enables local privilege escalation via the AF_ALG cryptographic interface and reportedly has public proof-of-concept code. Exploitation requires local, low-privileged access, and B&R reported no evidence of active exploitation at publication — but on process-control and edge devices, a local-to-root path is a meaningful stepping stone. Apply the updated APROL AutoYaST DVD build when available and enforce strict access control on all Linux-based B&R systems in the meantime.

CISA echoed a cluster of Siemens ProductCERT advisories on 23 June, several flagged for use in the energy sector. The headline item is a critical authenticated command-injection flaw in SINEC INS (fixed in V1.0 SP2 Update 6) that can allow arbitrary code execution, alongside information-disclosure, privilege-escalation and password-exposure issues in the same product. Map the advisories to your installed base and prioritize the SINEC INS command-injection and the protection-relay DoS.

Forescout: record ICS vulnerability volume in 2025, with most advisories rated high or critical

New Forescout research finds ICS vulnerability disclosure hit a record in 2025 — 508 advisories covering 2,155 CVEs, the highest since tracking began, averaging 4.2 vulnerabilities per advisory. The bigger concern for defenders is severity and visibility: roughly 82% of 2025's advisories were high or critical, and a large share of high/critical issues on critical Purdue Level 1–3 devices arrived without an accompanying CISA ICS advisory, leaving asset owners with blind spots. The takeaway is that advisory feeds alone won't surface everything — accurate asset inventory and exposure management remain the foundation for prioritizing the flood.

The revised NERC CIP-012-2 standard becomes enforceable on 1 July, now one day out, strengthening requirements to protect the confidentiality and integrity of real-time assessment and monitoring data exchanged between control centers and closing gaps the earlier version left around how that inter-control-center traffic is secured. For bulk-electric-system operators it is an immediate compliance milestone; for everyone else it is a useful reminder that control-center-to-control-center links are a prime target and deserve encryption, integrity protection and monitoring regardless of regulatory scope.
