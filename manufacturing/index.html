PTC Windchill RCE under active exploitation — the PLM backbone that holds your engineering drawings and pivots toward the shop floor

On 25 June CISA added CVE-2026-12569 (CVSS 9.3), an unauthenticated remote-code-execution flaw in PTC Windchill PDMLink/FlexPLM, to its KEV catalog, and attackers have kept dropping web shells onto unpatched, internet-reachable instances since. Windchill is the PLM system of record for engineering drawings, bills of material and supplier data at discrete manufacturers, and it integrates with MES, ERP and the engineering workstations that hold controller project files — so a compromise is an intellectual-property event, a supply-chain event and a credible pivot toward OT all at once. Patch immediately, hunt for unexpected JSP files and web shells, remove internet exposure of the PLM tier, rotate credentials, and review what the PLM environment can reach — and what can reach it.

Tata Electronics breach spills supplier and product data — leaked files reportedly include unreleased iPhone 18 Pro manufacturing details

Tata Electronics, the Indian contract manufacturer that builds for Apple and supplies Tesla, confirmed on 22 June that attackers breached its systems, and by 30 June reporting indicated the leaked trove included manufacturing data for unreleased Apple products — component schematics, PCB designs and material specifications attributed to the iPhone 18 Pro ahead of its expected September launch. The extortion group WorldLeaks — assessed as a rebrand of Hunters International that skips encryption and operates on pure data theft — claimed the attack on 12 June, posting more than 200,000 files it says total over 630 GB, including Outlook correspondence, SAP-related material and customer-linked documents. Tata Electronics said operations were unaffected and has since restricted remote access to critical internal systems such as purchase-order processing. For manufacturers the lesson is uncomfortable: your security perimeter now includes every contract manufacturer holding your drawings, and encryption-free extortion never trips the “ransomware” alarms that OT teams monitor for. Review what design and process data sits with suppliers and CMs, contractually require breach notification and data-handling controls, and monitor leak sites for your own part numbers and schematics.

Ransomware halts Australia's second-largest sugar producer at the start of crushing season — two mills down, 1,300+ growers idled

A 10 June ransomware attack on Mackay Sugar, Australia's second-largest raw sugar producer, shut down milling and cane haulage at its Farleigh and Racecourse mills just as the crushing season began — the annual window in which a year's cane crop must be processed, making downtime existential rather than inconvenient for the 1,300-plus family farms feeding the mills. The company brought in cybersecurity specialists, notified authorities, and reported partial restoration with limited manual operations at Farleigh by 15 June; on 16 June The Gentlemen ransomware group claimed the attack and threatened to leak stolen data. The Gentlemen — active since mid-2025 and among the top five groups in Dragos' Q1 2026 industrial ransomware analysis (see research card) — is known for tailored attack chains and double extortion. The seasonal-manufacturing lesson generalizes: attackers time impact to when you can least absorb it — harvest, peak season, product launch. Pressure-test whether your plants can run degraded or manual when IT dies, and pre-stage recovery for the systems whose loss stops the line.

Dragos Q1 2026 ransomware analysis: 1,020 industrial victims — and IT outages keep cascading into OT downtime

Dragos' industrial ransomware analysis for the first quarter of 2026 counted 1,020 ransomware incidents impacting industrial organizations worldwide, holding at the elevated baseline established in late 2025. Manufacturing again absorbed the largest share — 633 victims, 62% of all observed industrial ransomware victims — with construction-related manufacturing, industrial-equipment producers and food & beverage makers hit hardest. Qilin and Akira generated the highest claim volumes, with The Gentleman, LockBit5.0 and Play rounding out the top five operations. Notably, Dragos observed no ransomware variants engineered to manipulate industrial control protocols; the damage mechanism remains indirect but reliable — loss of ERP platforms, virtualization infrastructure, identity services and remote-access gateways cascading into OT disruption, safety risk and extended downtime. For manufacturers the numbers are the argument: if 62% of industrial ransomware lands on your sector, resilience of the IT tier your lines depend on is not an IT problem — it is the production-continuity problem.

Mitsubishi MELSOFT Update Manager ships with outdated 7-Zip — the update tool on engineering workstations becomes the attack vector

CISA advisory ICSA-26-181-01 (30 June) warns that Mitsubishi Electric's MELSOFT Update Manager (SW1DND-UDM-M) — the utility that keeps MELSOFT engineering software current on plant engineering workstations — bundles an outdated 7-Zip build carrying known vulnerabilities, including an integer-underflow flaw (CVE-2024-11477, CVSS 8.1) that can lead to code execution when a specially crafted archive is decompressed. The irony is the point: the tool trusted to deliver software updates becomes a delivery mechanism for compromise, on exactly the machines that hold MELSEC PLC project files and controller access across discrete-manufacturing lines. Multiple versions are affected — consult the advisory for the exact ranges — and Mitsubishi has released an updated version that remediates the bundled component. Update MELSOFT Update Manager itself, restrict what archives get opened on engineering workstations, and treat EWS as tier-0 OT assets: application allow-listing, no email/web browsing, and monitored file transfer only.

Critical Advisory

Max-severity SimpleHelp auth-bypass stays actively exploited past its federal KEV deadline — the remote-support tool vendors use to reach your OT

CISA added CVE-2026-48558, a CVSS 10.0 authentication-bypass flaw in the SimpleHelp remote monitoring and management (RMM) platform, to its Known Exploited Vulnerabilities catalog on 29 June with a federal remediation deadline of 2 July — a deadline that has now passed while exploitation continues. On servers configured for OpenID Connect the software fails to verify identity-token signatures, so an unauthenticated attacker can forge a token and be granted a fully authenticated “Technician” session — administrative control of the remote-access server, in some configurations bypassing MFA. The flaw is already being used to drop two newly identified malware families, the TaskWeaver loader and the Djinn infostealer, with roughly 14,000 SimpleHelp servers online and an estimated 1,000 directly vulnerable. For manufacturers the relevance is direct: RMM platforms are how machine builders, system integrators and service contractors reach production cells and packaging lines for remote support, and a compromised RMM server is a pre-authenticated path onto the shop floor. Patch to the fixed release immediately, pull the console off the public internet, rotate technician credentials and OIDC secrets, and hunt RMM-connected endpoints for TaskWeaver/Djinn activity and unexpected sessions.

Critical Advisory

Delta Electronics DVP12SE PLC exposes unauthenticated Modbus — anyone on the network can rewrite control logic, and no patch yet

CISA advisory ICSA-26-181-07 (30 June) warns that the Delta Electronics DVP12SE programmable logic controller exposes its Modbus TCP service on port 502 with no authentication or access control, so any reachable network source can read and write coils, holding registers, relay states and process-control functions without credentials or operator approval (CVE-2026-12818, CVE-2026-12819). A companion resource-exhaustion issue lets an attacker flood the Modbus port to degrade the device. Delta PLCs of this class are ubiquitous in discrete manufacturing — conveyor, packaging and machine-level control, frequently inside OEM-supplied equipment — often on flat cell networks where anything can reach port 502. All versions are affected and Delta is still working on a fix, so mitigation is compensating controls: enable the PLC's built-in IP Filter, segment and firewall the controller off any untrusted network, and never expose Modbus/502 to the internet.

Progress Kemp LoadMaster pre-auth RCE now seeing exploitation attempts — the load balancer that fronts enterprise and OT-facing services

eSentire's Threat Response Unit reported that CVE-2026-8037 (CVSS 9.8), a pre-authentication OS command-injection flaw in Progress Kemp LoadMaster, began drawing active exploitation attempts on 29 June — days after a public proof-of-concept and detailed write-up landed. The bug sits in the appliance's API: unsanitized input to the “/accessv2” endpoint lets an unauthenticated attacker inject commands and run code as root. The same defect affects other Progress ADC products (ECS Connection Manager, ObjectScale Connection Manager and the MOVEit WAF). eSentire noted the attempts it saw failed and produced no post-compromise activity, but with a working PoC circulating, successful exploitation is expected to follow. LoadMaster and ADC gear commonly sits at the network edge in front of web, VPN, remote-access and MES-adjacent services at plants — a pre-auth root takeover there is a strong pivot point toward internal and control networks. Apply Progress's fixed release now, restrict management/API access, and pull the appliance off any untrusted network.

On-prem SharePoint RCE jumps into CISA KEV after real-world attacks — the intranet backbone many plants still run in-house

CISA added CVE-2026-45659, a remote-code-execution flaw in on-premises Microsoft SharePoint Server (CVSS 8.8, caused by deserialization of untrusted data), to its Known Exploited Vulnerabilities catalog on 1 July after confirming active exploitation, with a federal remediation deadline of 4 July. The bug lets an authenticated user holding only Site Member permissions run code on the server — notably, Microsoft had rated exploitation “less likely” when it patched the issue back in May, so any organization that deferred the fix on that basis should re-prioritize now. On-premises SharePoint is a workhorse across manufacturing and engineering-firm IT, holding drawings, procedures and project data and often bridging toward OT-adjacent file shares and identity systems. Storm-2603, the actor behind Warlock ransomware, has been exploiting on-prem SharePoint since mid-2025, so this is also a ransomware precursor. Apply the May 2026 SharePoint update immediately, restrict and monitor authenticated access, and treat any exposed on-prem farm as a candidate pivot point toward OT-supporting IT.

High Advisory

New “CitrixBleed-pattern” NetScaler flaw exploited within 24 hours of disclosure — memory leakage from remote-access gateways

Citrix shipped fixes this week for six NetScaler ADC / NetScaler Gateway vulnerabilities, and the headline one — CVE-2026-8451 (CVSS 8.8), an insufficient-input-validation bug that causes a memory overread in the appliance's SAML handling — was being exploited in the wild less than 24 hours after public disclosure. Researchers at watchTowr showed that a malformed SAML authentication request padded with whitespace forces the parser to read past its buffer and return adjacent process memory to the client, echoing the CitrixBleed flaws whose leaked session tokens fueled major intrusions in 2023–24; honeypot operators observed live exploitation attempts beginning 30 June. Exposure requires the appliance to be configured as a SAML identity provider, which narrows the affected population but does not diminish the pattern. NetScaler gateways front VPN and remote-access services at plants and corporate manufacturing networks that bridge toward OT, and leaked memory can include material that lets attackers hijack authenticated sessions. Apply the fixed builds now, confirm whether any NetScaler is configured for SAML, terminate active sessions and rotate credentials after patching, and watch authentication logs for anomalous SAML requests.

Active — exploitation began <24h after disclosure Not in CISA KEV (as of this edition)
