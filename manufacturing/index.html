Ransomware halts all US production at Coca-Cola's Fairlife dairy

A ransomware attack forced Coca-Cola's Fairlife dairy subsidiary to suspend all US production on 16 July, a stark reminder that on the plant floor an IT compromise quickly becomes an operations problem. Coca-Cola disclosed in an SEC filing that intruders reached systems including production-related infrastructure, prompting a precautionary shutdown of Fairlife's US facilities while Canadian operations continued. The company says there is no indication product quality or safety was affected and is working with external experts and law enforcement. Whether encryption crossed into OT or the halt was purely precautionary, the outcome is identical: idle lines, spoiled perishables and disrupted supply. Manufacturing remains the most-targeted sector for industrial ransomware in 2026, and food and beverage — with just-in-time, perishable output — offers attackers exactly the operational leverage that makes a fast payout more likely.

High Incident — Nation-State

Iran-affiliated actors are rewriting plant-floor PLC logic and disabling safety functions

The updated federal advisory AA26-097A (22 July) confirms Iran-affiliated actors reaching internet-exposed PLCs — Rockwell CompactLogix and Micro850, Siemens S7-1200, Schneider Modicon M340 — extracting project files and modifying or deleting control logic, including Add-On Instructions, and manipulating HMI/SCADA displays. In some cases they disabled critical shutdown and alarm logic, letting equipment enter unsafe conditions without operator notification. For manufacturers that is the nightmare case: an engineering workstation or exposed controller becomes a path to silently altered process logic and defeated interlocks.

FortiSandbox RCE flaws added to CISA KEV — exploited appliances on the IT/OT boundary

CISA added two critical Fortinet flaws, CVE-2026-39808 and CVE-2026-25089 (both CVSS 9.1), to the KEV catalog on 16 July after in-the-wild exploitation. They are OS command-injection bugs in FortiSandbox / Cloud / PaaS enabling unauthenticated command execution via crafted HTTP requests. In plants, Fortinet appliances commonly sit on the boundary between enterprise IT and the production network; a compromised inspection appliance is both a foothold and a blind spot into OT. CISA set a 19 July federal remediation deadline.

Medium Threat Intel — Ransomware

Manufacturing stays the #1 ransomware target as RaaS groups industrialize

Industry tracking through the first half of 2026 keeps manufacturing at the top of the industrial ransomware table — the majority of observed industrial victims — driven by ransomware-as-a-service crews such as Qilin, SafePay and Akira running double-extortion playbooks. The recurring tradecraft is unglamorous: stolen or valid credentials, exposed remote-access services, lateral movement over common enterprise protocols, and data theft before encryption. The Fairlife halt is one visible instance of a pattern that hits food and beverage, industrial-equipment and construction-related manufacturers hardest.

July ICS Patch Tuesday: Siemens ships a CVSS-10 auth-bypass among nine advisories

July’s ICS Patch Tuesday saw Siemens, Schneider Electric and Rockwell publish fixes across their product lines. Siemens alone issued nine advisories including several criticals — a CVSS-10 token-invalidation/authentication-bypass flaw in Opencenter X that grants full application access, plus criticals affecting Mendix, SIMATIC S7-1500, Cadra and Desigo CC. Many stem from third-party components and enable code execution, denial of service, data exposure or privilege escalation. No public exploitation is reported, but the CVSS-10 auth bypass warrants prompt attention on affected deployments.

Unauthenticated debug port in a Rockwell adapter could hand attackers plant-floor control

Reporting in mid-July described an unauthenticated debug port in a Rockwell Automation adapter that could give an attacker on the network direct control at the plant-floor level. Adapters and communication modules are exactly the quiet, rarely-patched components that bridge controllers and networks, so an authentication gap there is high-consequence even without a dramatic CVSS headline. Affected operators should confirm the advisory against their hardware and apply the vendor’s guidance.
